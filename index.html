I am a Senior Crypto & Security architect at NXP Semiconductors in Eindhoven, the Netherlands.

Before that I was a postdoctoral researcher in the Digital Security group at Radboud University in the Netherlands. There I also obtained my PhD with Prof. Lejla Batina with my thesis titled Security on the Line: Modern Curve-based Cryptography. During the summers of 2016, 2017 and 2018 I was an intern in the Security and Cryptography group at Microsoft Research in Redmond. I completed Part III of the Mathematical Tripos at the University of Cambridge after receiving my BSc in Mathematics at Utrecht University.

I have a strong interest in developing cryptographic primitives and protocols. In particular I enjoy working on elliptic curves, both classically as well as in post-quantum settings, and I am one of the developers of the isogeny-based NIST proposal SIKE. My current focus is more generally on enabling post-quantum secure cryptography on resource-constrained systems to allow for timely migration under the looming quantum threat.
